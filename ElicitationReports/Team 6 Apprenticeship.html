Apprenticeship

Overview of the Technique

Apprenticing as an elicitation technique involves a designer and a customer (someone whose current needs would be met by the software system the designer is working on). The designer watches the customer as they perform their tasks and analyzes how they can design the software to improve the workflow. According to Holtzblatt and Beyer, the master-apprentice relationship works well for learning the structure of a system because “As they work, the structure implicit in the work becomes apparent [because] both master and apprentice are paying attention to it. It is easy for the master to pause and make an observation or for the apprentice to ask a question about an action. Observation interspersed with discussion requires little extra effort on the part of either master or apprentice [1].”

While a normal implementation of apprenticing would involve personally watching the process of potential users do the things the project hopes to better facilitate, restrictions in place due to COVID-19 complicate both the ability of the researchers to personally observe these users and the ability of users to actually accomplish the sort of tasks the project is focused on (namely, planning or seeking out local events or making plans with family and friends). Therefore, the technique outlined and carried out in this report will be a modified version of apprenticeship that does not conflict with current local health and safety guidelines, but attempts to capture as much of the same value that a regular approach to apprenticing would yield.

This report will detail the purpose and goals of apprenticeship as a requirements elicitation technique and the plan for implementing this technique. It will also summarize patterns found in the responses of volunteer participants in the research which will give an idea of what potential users currently do in their activity planning and also what they might expect or want from an Activities app.

Apprenticeship: Plan

Purpose

The purpose of this apprenticeship exercise is to gain a better understanding of how the users of this application currently plan and attend events [1]. The idea behind this is to learn how the users go about planning events so that the application can be designed in a way that is easy to understand and makes it easier for the users to plan and attend events with friends.

Technique

Due to COVID-19 we cannot implement the technique as usual. Because of this we have decided to ask a series of ten questions to determine how the customers currently work with planning events and activities.

Questions:

  • How do you find out about local activities?
  • Can you show me how you find local activities?
  • How do you plan events with friends and family?
  • Can you show me how you plan events with friends and family?
  • How do you find out about events with friends and family?
  • How do you coordinate the details for events with friends and family?
  • How often are you attending events that are planned by you or others each week?
  • How long on average are the events that you attend?
  • When you would like to attend an activity or even how do you find people to go with you?
  • How do you meet new people who share your interests to go to events with?

Correlated Data

How do you find out about local activities?

Those that were asked this question for almost all participants said that they find out about activities through word of mouth and through social media.

Can you show me how you find local activities?

Most did not answer this question or answered not applicable because they answered word of mouth to the previous question. Those that answered typically used Social media app or a messaging app to find the local activities.

How do you plan events with friends and family?

Everyone asked this question all said that they use text messaging to plan events with friends and family. Other answers include phone calls, face to face planning and group messages.

Can you show me how you plan events with friends and family?

Not much can be shown for this question.

How do you find out about events with friends and family?

Most people answered this question saying that they are informed about events with friends and family through group messaging. Other answers to this question included finding out in person, phone call, and on social media.

How do you coordinate the details for events with friends and family?

The most common way to coordinate the details of the event that we notice from the people we worked with is through text message or group chat on social media. A few people also mentioned calling as well.

How often are you attending events that are planned by you or others each week?

The specific average of time attending events each week is twice a week. A lot of people mentioned twice and three times too, being three times the second most common.

How long on average are the events that you attend?

The data shows that the events usually take two or three hours.

When you would like to attend an activity or even how do you find people to go with you?

Most of them use social media, specially Facebook, to find friends posting an invitation of messaging their most close friends directly.

How do you meet new people who share your interests to go to events with?

We had two similarities for this answer. First similarity is that people usually meet friends though the friends that they already have. Second similarity, some people are just not interested in making new friends so they do no really have a pattern for that.

Conclusion

This report detailed what apprenticing is, how our team executed apprenticing, the data that we collected, and the information that we found. Apprenticing is meant to gain a better understanding of the environment that we are creating an application for and in this case, we did gain new insights and information pertaining to how events are planned and carried out. With our collected data, we should be able to create an application that makes it faster and easier for users to plan and attend events and meet new people.

The data collected from this apprenticing technique is meant to help create an application that better suits the needs of the future users. From this technique the team was able to discern that there are many ways to create and plan events, however there are some common factors that are of importance. For example, many of the people that we interviewed used social media platforms like Facebook to find out about events. Another example is that many people would use group chats and would often spend an hour or more at events. These all show some very important tactics and ethics people use to plan out events. With this information we can add further requirements to the project, and create an application that becomes the preferred way of planning events.
