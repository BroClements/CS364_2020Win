Laddering Technique Report

Overview of the Technique

Technique: Laddering

In the laddering technique, the stakeholders are asked a sequence of questions that are expected to be answered clearly. The questions are organized in a systematic format, which will be required to have the collected answers to be organized in a structured way. The stakeholders must express their knowledge about the domain for this technique to be successful. Finally, by using the “Why set of questions,” the purpose of this technique is to clarify the requirement and understand the core values of the stakeholders regarding the project.

Pros Regarding Laddering:

  • This technique provides personal contact with stakeholders by trying to understand and prioritize their core values.
  • This technique collects essential requirements from the stakeholders and organizes it into a clear way for interpretation.

Cons Regarding Laddering:

  • When many questions are applied, the process can become ineffective by having the stakeholders feeling overleaped with an innumerous amount of, “why questions.”
  • Maintaining this technique can become challenging when requirements are added in and removed from the laddering process.

Application to the Project:

The team has studied the stakeholder’s Local Activity App Description and identified features described in its description. The team has met together and organized a structured ladder interview to identify the core value of the stakeholder and the final desires regarding those features. Finally, the team has met with the stakeholder and conducted an interview to collect the information.

Collected Data

We plan to use a pre-created template to record the information that we obtain in the interview. This template will be structured so that each construct is viewed in hierarchical order with arrows starting at the lowest level of abstraction and pointing to the highest level of abstraction. At each construct level, the stakeholder will give a score of 1-5, 1 being least important and 5 being most important, of how important each item is to them. This information will be compiled into a formal document after the interview is conducted.

Technique

Present requirements/features that we have identified from the project description. Check to see if there are misunderstandings or missing information.

List of Base Features:

  • Businesses can list available activities
  • Users can create their own activities
  • Users can plan an activity as friends or as a date
  • Users have control of the activities that they create.
  • Users can specify the group size of activities they make.
  • Users can see activities created by other users.
  • Users can request to join existing activities.

Steps

  1. Start with three features that we want to ladder up from.
  2. Ask a “why” question to discover the core values of the stakeholder.
  3. Take the stakeholder’s answer and make a construct to form the basis of the next “why” question.
  4. Continue this process until we reach the highest level of abstraction
