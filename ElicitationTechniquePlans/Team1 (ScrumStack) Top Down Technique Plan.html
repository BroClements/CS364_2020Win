Technique Plan

Elicitation Technique: Top Down

Technique Description:

The Top-Down Approach is a technique that produces specific requirements by breaking down a system to understand the details of a requirement. To begin, you start with some general or high-level features or requirements and then systematically brakes them down into increasingly smaller and more specific requirements. This assures that the resultant requirements are all focused on the main goals, and are specific enough to be easily implemented.

Purpose

This technique will provide a list of requirements in an ordinary matter from most general to most specific. The implementation of this technique will benefit the project by finding the core requirements in the total scope of the project.

Carrying out the technique

  1. Identify a minimum of three high-level requirements
  2. Analyze requirements, breaking them down into more basic components
  3. Continue breaking down components until the most basic attributes are identified and collect the data from them
  4. Record into a word document and report findings

Data Analysis

We plan to review each other team’s elicitation techniques in order to develop a list of starting general requirements. We will break each of these starting points into smaller, more specific requirements. The requirements will be focused on “why” and “what.”

Follow Up

After implementing the plan and recording the data. The team will analyze the data and format it in an easy and understandable format.
